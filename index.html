Guazábara TTRPG

Guazabara is a battle system for BoneDust RPG, designed to recreate the feel of life-or-death skirmishes in a narrative-focused environment. The rules focus on simplicity and tactical engagement with high stakes in combat.

Basic Combat Rules

  • Automatic Hits: In Guazabara, every attack automatically hits. Instead of rolling to hit, players roll for damage.
  • Critical Hits & Fumbles: If a player rolls maximum damage, they score a critical hit. If they roll a 1 on damage, it results in a fumble.
  • Armor: Armor in Guazabara reduces the damage taken from attacks. The armor’s reduction value is subtracted from the damage before it is applied to the character’s HP.

Multi-Attacks

When multiple characters attack the same target, each rolls their damage dice and keeps the highest result.

This rule simulates the intensity of group attacks, where a swarm of combatants could overwhelm a single foe.

Combat Phases

  • Declare Actions: At the start of each combat round, players declare their actions. This could include moving, attacking, casting a spell, or other combat actions.
  • Roll Initiative: Players roll a d20 for initiative. Actions are resolved in order of initiative, from highest to lowest.
  • Resolve Actions: Actions are then resolved according to the declared intent and rolled damage.
  • Apply Damage: Damage is applied after accounting for armor and other effects. If a critical or fumble occurs, narrate its dramatic effects.

Combat Maneuvers

In addition to basic attacks, characters can attempt the following combat maneuvers:

  • Disarm: Roll a d20. If successful (under the related stat), the enemy drops their weapon.
  • Grapple: Roll a d20. Success allows a character to grapple an enemy, pinning them and preventing action.
  • Push: Attempt to push the target, forcing them to move back or fall. Success is determined by a roll against the target's Might or Agility.

Morale & Retreat

At the GM's discretion, when enemies lose half their numbers or the leader falls, they must make a morale check. Failing the check may cause them to flee the battle or surrender.
